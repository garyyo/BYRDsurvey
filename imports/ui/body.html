Greetings from Kentucky! Thank you for visiting our site.

Why am I here?

You are here to help a computer learn how experts in Indo-European Studies describe how sounds change from PIE to Proto-Germanic.

Your responses will help to make the first reconstruction puzzle possible for the video game Roots of the Ancients (ROTA). ROTA is designed to provide a straightforward, engaging way to introduce middle schoolers (10-13) to the idea of Proto-Indo-European language and culture. If you would like to play a brief demo or watch a video of gameplay, click on this link.

Why are the Proto-Indo-European / Proto-Germanic reconstructions not what I expect?

Sometimes we had to fudge a Proto-Germanic form or a Proto-Indo-European form. Do not fret! This does not mean that those particular forms will be in the final game. The unexpected form is there for one of two reasons:

  1. We cannot include exceptional changes, which is why ‘five’ is reconstructed in PGmc. as *finhʷe, not *fimfe (or similar).
  2. We must start with the same form for all daughter branches. Analogy or paradigmatic levelling cannot be assumed.

All of this is due to how the machine learns -- the machine cannot learn the correct changes if we give it exceptional data.
